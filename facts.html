Extreme Conditions

Mount Everest is one of the most extreme environments on Earth. Temperatures can drop below -60°C, and winds can exceed 200 miles per hour. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}

At high altitudes, oxygen levels are extremely low—only about one-third of what is available at sea level. This makes breathing difficult and can lead to altitude sickness.

Geological Facts

Mount Everest continues to grow slightly every year due to tectonic activity caused by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian plates. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}

The mountain is estimated to be between 50 and 60 million years old, making it relatively young in geological terms.

Wildlife and Environment

Lower regions of Everest are home to animals such as snow leopards, red pandas, and Himalayan yaks. However, above 20,000 feet, almost no life can survive due to the extreme conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
