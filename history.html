The First Ascent

The first successful climb of Mount Everest took place in 1953 by Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay. Their journey marked one of the greatest achievements in human exploration.

Before this historic climb, many expeditions failed due to lack of equipment, knowledge of altitude sickness, and extreme weather conditions.

Modern Expeditions

Today, climbing Everest has become more accessible thanks to modern technology, guided expeditions, and improved equipment. However, it still remains extremely dangerous.

The mountain has seen overcrowding, environmental concerns, and increased risks, making it both a symbol of human ambition and a reminder of nature’s power.
