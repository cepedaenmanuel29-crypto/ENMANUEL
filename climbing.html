The Challenge of Climbing Everest

Climbing Mount Everest is one of the most difficult and dangerous challenges in the world. Climbers must endure extreme cold, strong winds, and very low oxygen levels.

The “Death Zone” begins at around 8,000 meters, where the human body cannot survive for long periods. At this altitude, even simple movements require great effort.

Main Dangers

  • Avalanches
  • Frostbite
  • Altitude sickness
  • Crevasses (deep cracks in ice)

Altitude sickness can cause confusion, weakness, and even death if not treated properly. Many climbers also suffer from frostbite due to extreme cold.

The Experience

Despite the dangers, climbers describe Everest as one of the most beautiful places on Earth. Standing at the summit allows you to see the curvature of the planet and endless mountain ranges.
