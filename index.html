The Highest Place on Earth

Rising above the clouds at 8,848.86 meters, Mount Everest stands as the highest point on Earth. Located in the Himalayas between Nepal and Tibet, it represents one of the most extreme environments ever explored by humans.

For centuries, the mountain remained untouched and unknown to the outside world. Today, it is both a scientific landmark and a symbol of human ambition.

A Mountain That Keeps Growing

Everest is not static. Due to the collision between the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, the mountain continues to rise a few millimeters every year.

This constant geological movement makes Everest a living structure, constantly reshaping itself over time.

More Than a Mountain

For climbers, Everest is not just a destination — it is a test of endurance, survival, and determination. Every ascent tells a story of risk, preparation, and respect for nature’s power.
