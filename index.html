About Mount Everest

Mount Everest is the highest mountain on Earth, reaching an elevation of approximately 8,848.86 meters (29,031 feet) above sea level. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1} Located in the Himalayas on the border between Nepal and Tibet, Everest is part of a massive mountain system formed over 50 million years ago when tectonic plates collided.

Known locally as "Sagarmatha" in Nepal, meaning "Peak of Heaven," and "Chomolungma" in Tibet, meaning "Goddess Mother of the World," Everest has deep cultural and spiritual significance for the people living nearby. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

Everest is not just a mountain—it is a global symbol of human ambition, exploration, and endurance. Every year, hundreds of climbers attempt to reach its summit, facing extreme weather conditions, freezing temperatures, and low oxygen levels.

Despite its beauty, Everest is also extremely dangerous. Climbers must deal with avalanches, powerful winds, and the “death zone,” an area above 8,000 meters where the human body begins to shut down due to lack of oxygen.
