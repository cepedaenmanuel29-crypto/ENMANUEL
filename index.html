Discover Everest

Mount Everest stands at 8,848 meters above sea level, making it the highest mountain on Earth. Located in the Himalayas, it forms a natural border between Nepal and Tibet. Everest is known not only for its height but for its extreme weather, powerful winds, and the dangerous “death zone” where oxygen levels are critically low.

Every year, hundreds of climbers attempt to reach its summit, facing avalanches, freezing temperatures, and unpredictable storms. Despite these dangers, Everest remains one of the most iconic and desired destinations for adventurers worldwide.
