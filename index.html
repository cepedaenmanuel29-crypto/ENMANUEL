Discover Mount Everest

Mount Everest rises 8,848.86 meters above sea level, making it the tallest mountain on Earth. It is part of the Himalayas and sits between Nepal and Tibet. Formed millions of years ago by tectonic plate collision, Everest continues to grow slowly every year.

Locally known as Sagarmatha and Chomolungma, the mountain holds deep cultural and spiritual meaning for the people of the region.

Why Everest Matters

Everest is not just a mountain—it represents human ambition, exploration, and survival. Every year, climbers attempt to reach its summit despite extreme danger, unpredictable weather, and the effects of altitude sickness.

  • Highest mountain on Earth
  • Extreme weather conditions
  • Symbol of human endurance
  • Located in the Himalayas

The Extreme Environment

Above 8,000 meters lies the “Death Zone,” where oxygen levels are so low that the human body begins to shut down. Temperatures can drop below -60°C, and winds can reach extreme speeds.

Climbers must use oxygen tanks, special gear, and carefully planned routes to survive. Even small mistakes can become life-threatening.
